my story

I'm Brandon! I'm a rising senior at Cupertino High School. I'm a brother, a boyscout, and a seeker.

But more importantly, I'm a tino student.

I've been at tino for the past 3 years and can confidently say that I've been through it all. I've cherished the countless hours that went into homecoming preparation, despised the days awake on 4 hours of sleep, and grown from the community we've made here. I take pride in our community. We've done some amazing things together, from raising over $13,000 for Hurricane Harvey relief back in 2017 to just this month when we granted Tony's Wish to go to Hawaii.

We each have our own strengths, weaknesses, and talents. We each have our own vision of how we want high school to turn out – or maybe we're discovering it as we go. Regardless, we each have a piece to share, something to say, or a change we want to see.

And that's why I'm proud of our tino community. It's not because we've raised over $20K in the past two years alone, that we're ranked 27th in the nation for STEM, or that we have some of the most brilliant students in the nation.

It's because despite our differences, unique backgrounds, and diverse beliefs, we're willing to share, listen, and come together.

For the following year, I want to focus on you, as an individual, a fellow student, but more importantly a member of our tino family.

the vision

Before we can aim for the skies, we have to lay our foundation on the ground. So let's jump in.

1. Improve communication and collaboration between ASB and campus organizations.

  • Streamline paperwork, forms, and application process for clubs
  • Work with ASB Treasurer to streamline financial processes
  • Create alternative fundraising opportunities to replace Clubs Day

2. Improve ASB transparency on two fronts.

  • First, increase financial visibility. Easily accessible public budget and spending spreadsheet.
  • Second, increase visibility on what happens in the class. Monthly recap meetings to not only highlight month's activities but a commissioner's contribution and work.

3. Implement an annual overarching goal.

  • Let me quickly explain this. We may take a quick detour, stop for a break, or lose or way every now and then, but a goal or vision keeps us in line. It reminds us why we're here and what we're working towards.
  • A school mission. It can be big, risky, maybe even a bit crazy. For instance, raising $50K for undeveloped schools.

4. Listen to what is being said.

  • Meet with club representatives every two months or after events to discuss or debrief.
  • Establish system with School Administrators to voice student's opinions.
  • Improve cooperation and communication between class council and ASB.

5. Continuing the legacy.

  • CHS has a long legacy of success with various events. We will build off what has been going well: Winter Formal, Homecoming, March Madness.
